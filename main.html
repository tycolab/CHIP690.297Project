Healthy Weight Management

Maintaining a healthy weight is an important part of overall health. It can help prevent and control many diseases and conditions. Achieving and maintaining a healthy weight requires a balanced approach, combining good nutrition, physical activity, and behavioral changes.

  • Focuses on lifelong habits, not temporary diets
  • Encourages mindful eating and portion control
  • Supports regular physical activity
  • Promotes setting realistic goals and tracking progress
  • Emphasizes building a supportive environment

Nutrition Basics

A healthy eating pattern supports both weight loss and long-term health. It should be rich in nutrients and low in added sugars, sodium, and unhealthy fats.

  • Fruits and vegetables: Aim for half your plate to be fruits and vegetables.
  • Whole grains: Choose brown rice, whole-wheat bread, and oatmeal over refined grains.
  • Lean proteins: Incorporate fish, poultry, beans, and nuts.
  • Healthy fats: Use olive oil, avocado, and nuts instead of butter or lard.
  • Low-fat dairy: Choose milk, yogurt, and cheese with reduced fat.

Tips: Plan meals ahead, read nutrition labels, and prioritize water over sugary drinks.

Setting Calorie Goals

Calorie needs vary based on age, sex, weight, height, and activity level. A calorie deficit — eating fewer calories than your body uses — leads to weight loss.

  • 1,200–1,500 calories/day: Often effective for weight loss in women.
  • 1,500–1,800 calories/day: Often effective for weight loss in men and active women.
  • Very low-calorie diets (<800 calories/day) should be supervised by a healthcare provider.

Building an Active Lifestyle

Physical activity helps maintain weight loss and provides additional health benefits like improved mood, better sleep, and stronger muscles.

Goal: 150–300 minutes of moderate-intensity exercise per week or 75–150 minutes of vigorous-intensity exercise per week.

Examples of Moderate Activities:
  • Brisk walking (2 miles in 30 minutes)
  • Gardening
  • Water aerobics
  • Bicycling at a moderate pace (5 miles in 30 minutes)
Examples of Vigorous Activities:
  • Running (1.5 miles in 15 minutes)
  • Swimming laps
  • Jumping rope
  • Playing basketball
Tips to Increase Daily Activity:
  • Take the stairs instead of the elevator.
  • Park farther away from entrances.
  • Take short walking breaks during the day.
  • Find activities you enjoy to stay motivated.

BMR Calculator

The Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) is the amount of energy needed while resting in a temperate environment when the digestive system is inactive. It is the amount of energy your body needs to maintain basic life functions at rest, such as breathing, circulation, and cell production. It represents the minimum number of calories your body needs to survive. Your BMR is calculated based on your age, gender, height, and weight using the Mifflin-St Jeor Equation, which is widely considered one of the most accurate methods for estimating BMR.

Behavior Strategies for Success

Changing habits takes time. These strategies can help you stay on track:

  • Track food intake and activity using an app or journal.
  • Set SMART goals (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound).
  • Find a support system among family or friends.
  • Anticipate setbacks and plan how you'll respond to challenges.

Even small changes can lead to big results over time. Focus on progress, not perfection.
